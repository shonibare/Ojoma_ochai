ABOUT

Ojoma Ochai is a Creative Economy expert with almost 20 years’ experience supporting the development of the global Creative Economy through contributions to various initiatives including Capacity Building, Technical Assistance, and funding initiatives that support global sector growth.

Ms Ochai sits on the UNESCO Global Expert Facility for the 2005 Convention on the Protection and Promotion of the Diversity of Cultural Expressions. In this capacity, she advices the multilateral agency and has supported national governments like The Gambia, Jamaica, and Nigeria to implement the convention and grow their Creative Economies. Currently the Managing Partner for the Creative Practice Ojoma leads the organisation to stimulate growth in the Creative Economies of the global south particularly in Africa

The practice does this by designing and delivering initiatives that contribute to:

  • Stronger ecosystems and ecosystem Players (in public and private sector) that better serve the needs of Creative Economy businesses / firms and professionals
  • Enhanced Investment Readiness of Creative Economy businesses/ firms
  • More and better-informed investors deploying appropriate capital into Creative Economy businesses / firms
    • Before this role, Ojoma Ochai was the Regional Director for Arts and Creative Economy Programmes for British Council in Sub Saharan Africa. In this role, she worked with colleagues across Sub- Saharan Africa and the UK to devise strategies and lead programme teams to deliver positive artistic, social, economic and Cultural Relations outcomes, particularly for young people working in the Arts, and the Creative and Digital Economies.

She has also worked on various international creative and digital sector development efforts for example for the World Bank Growth and Employment Project on Music and Film Sector Development in Nigeria and with the Swedish Arts Council on Artistic Freedom. She has spoken around the world on trends and opportunities in the African Creative and Digital Industries. These speaking engagements have included: the 8th World Summit on Arts and Culture, Malaysia, World Press Freedom Day, Ghana and the 1st OECD summit on Culture and Development in Italy. She has also spoken in UK parliament about opportunities for UK – Nigeria collaboration in the Creative Economy

Nominated as Young Person of the year by the Future Awards in 2010, Ojoma is a Fellow of the DEVOS Institute of Arts Management, University of Maryland, an Associate Fellow of the Nigerian Leadership Institute and sits on the board of the African Technology and Creative Group.
